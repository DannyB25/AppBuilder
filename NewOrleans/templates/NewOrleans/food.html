Welcome to the Hospitality Page!

On this webpage, we will explore a few of the popular New Orleans restaurants and haunts where you can find some of the best eats and drinks in the country! Please read through to find some spots you won't want to miss on your next trip to The Big Easy!

Buffa's Lounge

"On the Border of the Quarter since 1939, Buffa's is the best place you've never been. The front bar is open 11AM to close, with additional seating outdoors and in our Back Room as needed. We have live music in our Back Room Thursday thru Monday. See our website for details."

Commander's Palace

"Commander's Palace, nestled in the middle of the tree-lined Garden District, has been a New Orleans landmark since 1893. Known for the award-winning quality of its food and its convivial atmosphere, the history of this famous restaurant offers a glimpse into New Orleans' storied past and has been the go-to destination for Haute Creole cuisine and whimsical Louisiana charm. The winner of seven James Beard Foundation Awards, Commander's Palace has evolved into a culinary legend." For more information, please visit their website here.

Ms. Mae's

"The Club Ms. Mae's is famously known as New Orleans' cheapest, 24-hour dive bar. It's a hole in the wall spot, but most locals say Ms. Mae's should be on everyone's bucket list. Located at the intersection of Magazine Street and Napoleon Avenue, you can stop in for a drink and a game of pool or air hockey any time of day (literally, anytime). If you're feeling extra adventurous and fall head-over-heels in love with the place, Ms. Mae's dares customers to complete the 24-hour challenge where bar goers must have a drink every hour, for a 24-hour stint!"
